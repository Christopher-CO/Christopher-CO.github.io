Project Summary

The Power Arm is a cabel-actuated robotic exoskeleton arm designed to augment user strength by up to 30 lbs. Bowden cables are used to transmit power from back-mounted high torque stepper motors that are regulated by a microcontroller and ratchet-pawl locking system. The device is remote controlled using input from a mobile GUI (or wireless remote), visulalizing in real-time the joint angles, power output, movement speed, and other parameters being processed by the microcontroller.
